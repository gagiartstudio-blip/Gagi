Overview

A mobile-first mental wellness ecosystem bridging the gap between acute stress and professional intervention through "Calm UI" principles and research-driven pathways. The project emerged from observing that one in five Indians may suffer from depression, yet only a fraction seek professional help — due to stigma, access barriers, and a shortage of trained professionals.

3

02

Challenge

Current mental health platforms often fail to bridge the gap between clinical rigour and daily user accessibility. Users in high-stress states face significant "analysis paralysis," where complex interfaces exacerbate the very anxiety they seek to alleviate.

"The challenge was to design a system that reduces cognitive load during acute episodes while maintaining the data integrity required for professional clinical follow-up. How do we make an app feel like a deep breath rather than another task?"

Key Friction Points

  1. 01 Cognitive overload during acute anxiety episodes.
  2. 02 Lack of immediate, safe venting mechanisms.
  3. 03 Overly clinical, alienating language and UI patterns.

Research Method

11 deep-dive semi-structured interviews across three user groups:

Students 5
Working professionals 5
Licensed counsellor 1
03

A human-centric research methodology mapped friction points across care journeys, then card sorting structured 60+ features into a coherent, navigable information architecture before a single wireframe was drawn.

04

Solution

The final interface focuses on 'Calm UI' principles — soft colour fields, reduced visual density, and empathetic micro-copy to lower user anxiety at every interaction point.

Principle 01

One-Action Architecture

Context-aware breathing tools and mood check-ins surfaced directly on the home screen based on detected user state — eliminating navigation friction during acute moments.

Principle 02

Low-Stimulus UI

High-saturation colours eliminated to prevent ocular fatigue during late-night distress. A muted, warm palette signals safety rather than engagement.

Principle 03 — Impact

+32%

User engagement via empathetic micro-copy

Empathetic Micro-copy

Reframing "Log Data" as "Share Your Mood" changed the emotional register of the app from clinical tool to trusted companion — a shift that measurably increased sustained engagement.

Therapy Session Ecosystem

05

Reflection

Working on mental health requires a shift from standard engagement-driven UX to wellness-driven UX. Success is defined by emotional stability, not time spent on the app.

Success

Minimalist navigation reduced "super-app" fatigue for users seeking quick relief.

Learning

Gamification streaks increase anxiety for users who feel pressure to maintain them.

Next Steps

Exploring AI journaling to automate emotional tagging for users who find writing difficult.
